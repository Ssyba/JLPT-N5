どなた (donata)

  • ど (do): This is a prefix used in many Japanese question words (e.g., どこ, どう, etc.).
  • なた (nata): This is a more polite version of "who."

しつれいですが
excuse me, but
Imagine a 'shit ray' (しつれい) gun that you reluctantly have to use. As you prepare to fire it, you say, 'This is gonna (ですが) be unpleasant. Excuse me, but I need to use my 'shit ray. And then you blast the bad guys but shit gets over everyone and now they hate you.
  1. しつれい (shitsurei): This part means "rude" or "impolite." It is often used in phrases to express politeness or apologize for any inconvenience.
    • しつ (shitsu): A shortened form, often seen in formal or compound words.
    • れい (rei): Courtesy or manners. Combined with しつ (shitsu), it forms the word for impoliteness.
  2. です (desu): This part is a copula, often translated as "is" or "am." It adds politeness and formality to the sentence.
  3. が (ga): This part is a conjunction meaning "but" or "however." It is used to soften the request or statement that follows, making it more polite.
When combined, しつれいですが (shitsurei desu ga) can be translated to "Excuse me, but..." or "I'm sorry to bother you, but..."
