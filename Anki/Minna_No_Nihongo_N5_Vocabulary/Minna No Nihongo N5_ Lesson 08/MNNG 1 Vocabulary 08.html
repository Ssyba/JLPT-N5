2
きれい(な)
beautiful, clean
- "Ki" (き) sounds like "key," representing something precious that you want to keep to yourself.
- "Rei" (れい) sounds like "ray," as in a ray gun named "Lovely," which embodies beauty.
So, imagine you're locking away something beautiful with a key, and you also have a ray gun named Lovely that shines with beauty. This helps you remember that "kirei" means "beautiful" or "pretty."
3
しずか(な)
quiet
Since this word consists of a kanji with hiragana attached, you can bet that it will use the kun'yomi reading. You didn't learn that reading with this kanji, so here's a mnemonic to help you: You need to be quiet. She's (しず) being nice and quiet, unlike you!
4
にぎやか(な)
lively
- "Nigi" sounds like "knee" and "guy.
- "Yaka" can be linked to "yelling" or loud sounds.
So, imagine a guy yelling in a busy, lively crowd, making the scene feel bustling and full of energy. This helps you remember that "nigiyaka" means "lively" or "bustling."
5
ゆうめい(な)
famous
- "Yuu" sounds like "you.
- "Mei" sounds like "may."
Imagine someone saying, "You may know them, they’re famous!" This ties the sound of "yumei" with the concept of fame, helping you remember that "yumei" means "famous."
6
しんせつ(な)
kind
- "Shin" sounds like the name Shin.
- "Setsu" can be linked to sets, like setting the table or doing something nice.
So, imagine someone named Shin kindly setting the table for a meal, which helps you remember that "shinsetsu" means "kind" or "gentle."
7
げんき(な)
healthy, sound, cheerful
- "Gen" sounds like a person's name, Gen.
- "Ki" sounds like key, as in the key to something.
So, imagine someone named Gen has a special key to staying healthy and energetic, which helps you remember that "genki" means "healthy" or "energetic."

ひま(な)
free(time)
In your spare time you like to drink hima (ひま), an off brand version of Zima.
9
べんり(な)
convenient
- "Ben" (べん) is associated with "bento," symbolizing something practical and useful.
- "Ri" (り) comes from "reed," further emphasizing convenience by creating easy-to-use products.
So, imagine a leader finding a bento and reeds that make everyone's life easier, helping you remember that "benri" means convenient!

おおきい

What do you do when you see something really, really big? You say おお because you can't believe your eyes. Imagine something really large and say おお out loud to help seal the memory in that brain of yours.

When you think of the word small, try to think of this cheese (ちい) store. You go inside, and all of their cheeses are so small. They hand you a magnifying glass to see all the cheeses. You look at them and exclaim: "Why are these cheeses so small???"

おもしろい

The reading is unlike what you've learned before, at least with the 面 part (白い is just like the vocab word 白い). When you look at the white face of the object you say to your friend Moe: "Oh mo!" (おも) because you want him to come over and look at this. Try to imagine someone you know named Moe, or at least a celebrity or something named Moe.

おいしい

The reading for this makes no sense at all. You'll have to learn it straight up, that is, unless you know the word おいしい already, which many people do as it's used in every other sentence in every Japanese variety show. If you don't know this word already, just think of yourself eating something delicious. It's so delicious it makes you mad, I have no idea why. You yell at the chef: "OI (おい). OI! WHY IS THIS SO DELICIOUS????"
